## The Dark Side of Plants: Chemical Warfare

Here's something that might blow your mind: **plants don't want to be eaten**. They can't run away, so they evolved chemical defenses. These are called antinutrients, and they're in almost everything you've been told to eat more of.

### **Phytates** (in grains, beans, nuts, seeds)
These bind to zinc, iron, calcium, and magnesium, making them impossible to absorb. They form insoluble complexes that pass right through you. Soaking and fermenting helps, but only reduces them by 50-70%.

**Translation:** You can eat "enough" minerals on paper and still be deficient because phytates are stealing them.

### **Oxalates** (in spinach, almonds, sweet potatoes, chard)
A 2018 study in *Kidney International* found that consuming over 200mg of oxalates daily increases kidney stone risk by 40%. 

Here's the kicker: **one cup of spinach has 750mg of oxalates**. The recommended safe limit? 50-100mg per day.

Spinach also has calcium, but the oxalates bind to it, so you absorb almost none. It's a nutritional trap.

### **Lectins** (in beans, grains, nightshades)
These can increase gut permeability and trigger inflammation. Pressure cooking reduces them but doesn't eliminate them.
