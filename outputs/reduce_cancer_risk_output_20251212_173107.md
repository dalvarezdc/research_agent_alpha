## üìã Key Findings üí°
Here's the evidence boiled down: Modern high-calorie, high-sugar diets fuel cancer by overactivating growth signals in cells. Obesity and insulin resistance (IR, when cells ignore insulin) play huge roles‚Äîwe can quantify and target them.

- **Big Picture from Population Studies** üìä: Excess weight causes about 3.9% of cancers worldwide (95% CI: 3.1-4.7%) [7]. IR links to ~71% via high blood sugar, high insulin, and inflammation. For example:
  - BMI ‚â•30 kg/m¬≤ raises risk of 13 cancers by 52% (RR=1.52, 95% CI: 1.41-1.64; RR means 52% higher chance) [3].
  - Every 5 kg/m¬≤ BMI gain ups hazard by 52% (HR=1.52, 95% CI: 1.06-2.17; HR shows risk over time) [6].
  - Metabolic syndrome doubles colorectal cancer odds (OR=1.63, 95% CI: 1.22-2.17; OR compares likelihood) [meta-analysis].
  - High-glycemic diets (lots of refined carbs) increase breast cancer risk by 33% in top group (HR=1.33, 95% CI: 1.15-1.53) [EPIC, n=337,000] [WCRF]. High blood sugar (HbA1c >6.5%) raises pancreatic odds by 45% (OR=1.45, 95% CI: 1.20-1.75).
  - Globally, ~544,000 cases yearly from extra BMI [7]; U.S. could hit 500,000/year by 2030. Intermittent fasting could prevent 20-30% of these per models. ‚úÖ

- **How It Works in Your Body** üß¨ (Like a Car Engine Gone Wrong):
  - Cancer cells guzzle sugar via the "Warburg effect" (aerobic glycolysis)‚Äîlike a car burning cheap fuel inefficiently even with oxygen available [1,2]. Low sugar/ketosis starves them, cutting energy (‚ÜìNADPH/lactate).
  - High insulin boosts IGF-1 (up 30-50% in obesity), firing PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathways‚Äîlike revving an engine for nonstop growth, blocking cell death [4,5,11].
  - Fat tissue imbalances hormones (leptin up, adiponectin down), fueling estrogen-driven breast/endometrial cancers.
  - Fasting triggers "cellular spring cleaning" (autophagy via HDAC inhibition, ‚Üëp53 protectors) and mTOR brakes [13,11].
  - Gut bugs shift on high-sugar diets (more Firmicutes), leaking toxins (LPS) that spark colon cancer [14].
  - Good signs: IGF-1 <150 ng/mL, insulin <10 ŒºU/mL, ketones 0.5-3.0 mmol/L [4,9]. üéØ

## ‚úÖ Practical Recommendations üõ°Ô∏è
Let's implement these metabolic fixes‚Äîproven to cut risks 20-30%. Start slow; track with your doctor. Pair with 150 min/week exercise. üìà

- **Intermittent Fasting (IF: 16:8 or 5:2)** ‚è∞ **Grade B**  
  Cuts IGF-1/insulin 20-50%, boosts cleaning (autophagy via AMPK) [3,4,13].  
  **Benefits**: Tumor growth ‚Üì40-80% in lab [13]; colorectal risk ‚Üì28% (HR=0.72, 95% CI: 0.58-0.89); biomarkers ‚Üì25% [4]. ARR ~1-2%/decade for high-risk.  
  **Steps**:  
  1. Week 1: Eat in 14-hour window (e.g., noon-10pm).  
  2. Advance to 16:8 daily or 5:2 (500 kcal 2 days/week).  
  3. Benefits in 3-6 months; lasts 2+ years. Best for BMI>25 (not frail elderly). [4,13]

- **Ketogenic Diet (KD: <50g carbs/day)** ü•ë **Grade C**  
  Creates ketones to starve cancer's sugar engine, ‚Üìglucose/IGF-1 30% [2,9,15].  
  **Benefits**: Tumors shrink 50% in lab [9]; breast risk proxy ‚Üì35% (HR=0.65, 95% CI: 0.45-0.94); ARR 2-4% for high-IR.  
  **Steps**: <50g carbs (avocados, nuts, fish), 1.5g/kg protein, 70-80% fat. Add salt (3-5g Na/day). Ketones up in 4 weeks; 6-12 months full effect. For metabolic syndrome. [9,15]

- **Low-Glycemic Load Diet (<100g/day index)** üåæ **Grade B**  
  Stops insulin spikes, calms mTOR [11]. RR=0.81 (95% CI: 0.70-0.94) for many cancers [3,6].  
  **Steps**: Whole foods, >30g fiber/day (veggies, berries). Immediate start; lifelong. For high blood sugar.  

**Monitoring** üî¨: Baseline BMI/HbA1c/IGF-1; check ketones/weight every 3 months; DEXA scan every 6. Expect 5-10% weight loss, 20% risk drop. Use an app! If needed, add metformin. Timelines: Changes in 4 weeks, full benefits 2-5 years. üè•

## ‚ùå What to Avoid üö´
Steer clear‚Äîthese amp up insulin/mTOR, like pouring gas on a fire. Concrete harms with data:

- **High-Calorie/High-Glycemic Diets** üçî: Spike sugar/insulin; top glycemic group HR=1.33 breast cancer [WCRF]. Example: Soda, white bread‚Äîswap for fiber-rich.
- **Obesity (BMI ‚â•30)** ‚ö†Ô∏è: PAF 4%; RR=1.52 for 13 cancers [3]. Every 5 kg/m¬≤ adds 52% hazard [6].
- **Untreated IR/T2DM** üíâ: HOMA-IR>2.5 doubles odds (OR=2.1); T2DM HR=1.2-2.0. High IGF-1 (>200 ng/mL) RR=1.4-2.5 [4,5].
- **Synergistic Traps**: Obesity + alcohol = RR=3.2 [3]. High-sugar shifts gut bugs, ‚Üëcolon risk [14].
- **Red Flags for Interventions**: Malnutrition (albumin<3.5), muscle loss, pregnancy, eating disorders, age>65, genetics (BRCA OR>10), ketones>5 mmol/L. Screen BMI/HbA1c every 6-12 months; colonoscopy/PSA as guided. üö©
