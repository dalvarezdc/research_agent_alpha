## 📋 Overview

### 🧬 Drug Classification
**Drug Class:** Biguanide antidiabetic agent

### 🔬 Mechanism of Action
Decreases hepatic glucose production by inhibiting gluconeogenesis, reduces intestinal absorption of glucose, and improves insulin sensitivity by increasing peripheral glucose uptake and utilization in muscle and adipose tissue.

---

## ⚗️ Pharmacology

### Absorption
Oral bioavailability is 50-60 percent with absorption occurring primarily in the small intestine via saturable active transport; peak plasma levels occur in 2-3 hours.

### Distribution & Metabolism
Not metabolized by the liver or any cytochrome P450 enzymes; remains unchanged in the body.

### Elimination
Excreted unchanged in urine via glomerular filtration and active tubular secretion with 90 percent renal clearance.

**Half-Life:** 4.5 to 6.2 hours in patients with normal renal function; prolonged in renal impairment.

## 💉 Clinical Use

### Approved Indications
1. Type 2 diabetes mellitus as monotherapy or combination therapy

### Off-Label Uses
1. Polycystic ovary syndrome to improve ovulation
2. Prevention of type 2 diabetes in high-risk individuals
3. Management of antipsychotic-induced weight gain

### Standard Dosing
Initial 500 mg twice daily with meals, titrated by 500 mg weekly to 1000 mg twice daily; maximum 2550 mg per day in divided doses.

### Dose Adjustments
**Renal Impairment:**
Reduce dose if eGFR 30-45 mL/min/1.73 m2; contraindicated below 30 mL/min/1.73 m2

**Hepatic Impairment:**
Use with caution; avoid in severe liver disease due to lactic acidosis risk

**Elderly:**
Initiate at 500 mg daily and titrate slowly based on renal function

## 🔗 Interactions

### 💊 Drug-Drug Interactions

#### 🟡 Lisinopril (MODERATE)

**Mechanism:** ACE inhibitor may reduce renal function leading to decreased metformin clearance and accumulation

**Clinical Effect:** Increased risk of lactic acidosis and hyperkalemia

**Management:** Monitor serum creatinine and eGFR at baseline and every 3 months; adjust metformin dose if eGFR declines

**Evidence Level:** moderate


#### 🔴 Iodinated contrast media (SEVERE)

**Mechanism:** Contrast-induced nephropathy reduces metformin excretion

**Clinical Effect:** Elevated risk of lactic acidosis

**Management:** Withhold metformin for 48 hours after contrast procedure and resume only after renal function is confirmed stable

**Time Separation:** 48 hours post-procedure

**Evidence Level:** strong


#### 🟡 Alcohol (MODERATE)

**Mechanism:** Alcohol inhibits gluconeogenesis and may potentiate metformin effects on lactate

**Clinical Effect:** Increased risk of hypoglycemia and lactic acidosis

**Management:** Limit alcohol intake to less than 2 drinks daily and monitor blood glucose

**Evidence Level:** moderate
