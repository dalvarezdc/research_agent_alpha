## 🧬 Pharmacology

### 🎯 Mechanism of Action
Inhibits cyclooxygenase (COX) enzymes, primarily COX-2, in the central nervous system, reducing prostaglandin synthesis from arachidonic acid to mediate analgesic and antipyretic effects with minimal peripheral anti-inflammatory activity.

### ⚗️ Pharmacokinetics
- **Absorption:** Rapidly absorbed from the gastrointestinal tract with bioavailability of 70-90%. Onset of action within 30-60 minutes and peak plasma concentrations at 1-2 hours.
- **Metabolism:** Primarily hepatic via glucuronidation (45-55%) and sulfation (25-35%); minor CYP2E1 pathway produces NAPQI metabolite.
- **Elimination:** Excreted primarily in urine as inactive conjugates; renal route predominates.
- **Half-Life:** 2-3 hours in healthy adults

**Dose Adjustments:**
- **Hepatic Impairment:** Reduce dose or avoid in severe hepatic impairment
- **Renal Impairment:** Extend dosing interval in severe renal impairment
