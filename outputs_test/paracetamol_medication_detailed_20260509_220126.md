## 📋 Overview

### 🧬 Drug Classification
**Drug Class:** Analgesic and antipyretic; para-aminophenol derivative

### 🔬 Mechanism of Action
Inhibits cyclooxygenase (COX) enzymes, primarily COX-2, in the central nervous system, reducing prostaglandin synthesis from arachidonic acid to mediate analgesic and antipyretic effects with minimal peripheral anti-inflammatory activity.

---

## ⚗️ Pharmacology

### Absorption
Rapidly absorbed from the gastrointestinal tract with bioavailability of 70-90%. Onset of action within 30-60 minutes and peak plasma concentrations at 1-2 hours.

### Distribution & Metabolism
Primarily hepatic via glucuronidation (45-55%) and sulfation (25-35%); minor CYP2E1 pathway produces NAPQI metabolite.

### Elimination
Excreted primarily in urine as inactive conjugates; renal route predominates.

**Half-Life:** 2-3 hours in healthy adults

## 💉 Clinical Use

### Approved Indications
1. Relief of mild to moderate pain
2. Reduction of fever

### Off-Label Uses
1. Osteoarthritis pain management
2. Postoperative analgesia

### Standard Dosing
Adults: 325-650 mg orally every 4-6 hours as needed, maximum 4 g daily.

### Dose Adjustments
**Hepatic Impairment:**
Reduce dose or avoid in severe hepatic impairment

**Renal Impairment:**
Extend dosing interval in severe renal impairment
